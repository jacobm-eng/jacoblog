Career Counseling High Schoolers During the Age of AI

April 8, 2026

On March 5, Anthropic published some research on the labor market impacts of AI at https://www.anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts. The article includes a graph illustrating the theoretical and observed capabilities for a variety of occupations. In the graph, it shows AI doing most of the "whitecollar" jobs. If you add into this graph those occupations that will probably be done by robots in the near future, there isn't much left for humans to do. Maybe there would be occupations for some certain high-dexterity skills and activites involving human-to-human personal care, like nursing. But, that's it.

If that is the approaching future, what do you advise high schoolers to train for, to aim for, in their quickly approaching work careers? This is a struggle that I've been trying to solve as father with a teenager.

I haven't had a good answer; so, I decided to act like an engineer and think through all the possible branches of what might happen and assign likelihoods. This is meant for the USA and is influenced by my personal biases. So, some of the likelihood estimates are probably off, but I think it is directionally correct. Here it is:

What do AI & robots do to work?

  1. [All] AI and robots completely take over all work (5% likelihood). This is the option is touted by some future-oriented techno-optimists.
    Are strong social welfare programs enacted?
    1. No (50%) - Techno-feudal dystopia future
    2. Yes (50%)
      Do the capitalists decamp to a jurisdiction with lower taxes/burdens/forced sharing?
      1. Yes (50%) - Techno-feudal dystopia future
      2. No (50%) - UBI/Wall-E utopia future
  2. [Partial] AI and robots take over some-to-many jobs, but not all. Occupations requiring human interaction or exploring novel environments with unknown win conditions remain (15%). This option is aligned with Anthropic's research work above about what is possible.
    Are strong social welfare programs enacted?
    1. No (70%) - Techno-feudal dystopia future
    2. Yes (30%)
      Do the capitalists decamp?
      1. Yes (50%) - Techno-feudal dystopia future
      2. No (50%) - UBI/Wall-E utopia future
  3. [Transformative] All jobs more or less remain, but AI and robots become like modern power tools transforming how those jobs are executed. (5%) This one aligns with the experience of computer programmers using Anthropic's coding assistant tools so far.
    Are strong social welfare programs enacted?
    1. No (80%) - Normalcy continues
    2. Yes (20%)
      Do the capitalists decamp?
      1. Yes (50%) - Techno-feudal dystopia future
      2. No (50%) - UBI/Wall-E utopia future
  4. [Mix] AI and robots eliminate some jobs and transform others. (75%) This is sort of the default option that recognizes that the only constant is change; and, AI and robots will change things, but it is hard to confidentally say they will elminiate ALL jobs.
    Are strong social welfare programs enacted?
    1. No (90%) - Normalcy continues
    2. Yes (10%)
      Do the capitalists decamp?
      1. Yes (50%) - Techno-feudal dystopia future
      2. No (50%) - UBI/Wall-E utopia future

I'll tally up the likelihoods in a moment, but I have a few comments before that. First, before this exercise, I was so focused on the utopia and dystopia outcomes that I hadn't thought much about normal life just continuing. Next, capitalists are known to move to avoid taxes or less forced sharing in response to government regulations in 20th and 21st Century history. Last, I don't think the catastrophe scenario where Terminators or grey goo or a supervirus happens, but if it does then career choices don't matter.

The three possible outcomes (Techno-fuedal dystopia, UBI/Wall-E utopia, and Normalcy continues) all interestingly deal with the same 3 aspects of life: freedom of choice to decide how you spend your time, the daily grind of survival, and whether or not there is a government-provided baseline to the needs of life.

In the Techno-feudal Dystopia, you no longer have freedom of choice for your occupation because AI and robots do all or nearly all of them. But, your daily grind of survival continues, and there is no government-provided baselines to meet your needs. Either you become a peasant foraging in the wreckage, or an entertaining servant, or maybe a henchman. This seems like a likely outcome if the rich hoard the technologies, but also if the capitalists flee paying for social welfare programs (and leave for Singapore/the Moon/the High Seas) because the authorities seize whatever assets are left and they effectively become the new feudal lords. Everywhere turns into Skid Row or Cuba circa 2026.

The above decision tree gives the Techno-feudal dystopia a 21% likelihood of happening. Honestly, that's higher than I'm comfortable with. The smart moves for this future seem to be to acquire and control the technology assets, but also it seems worthwhile to foster your local community so that you don't need to decamp or hire henchmen.

The UBI/Wall-E Utopia is nearly the exact opposite of the Techno-feudal dystopia. In the utopia, you still have freedom of occupational choice, but you no longer have to struggle through the daily grind of survival because the government is providing a baseline to meet needs. Food, shelter, clothing, energy, Internet, transportation, health care, and educational needs are basically met. You have time to pursue self-actualization and can seek private funding for bigger projects, but the big danger is complacency and all the problems that come with that - like the the floating chair humans in Wall-E.

The utopia has a slim 8% chance of happening in the decision tree. The smart moves to prepare for this future seem to be to stay alive and to also stay disciplined to strive for your dreams, whatever those may be and however they may change.

The last potential future was the one that I was certain was no longer an option. In the Normalcy Continues, you have the freedom to pursue your choice of occupation, but the grind of daily survival still exists because the government provides no (or a very limited) baseline for meeting people's needs. Robots and AI are around in the workplace. A few jobs have ended, but many more have transformed.

Normalacy, even though it happens on just 2 of the branches, has a 71% chance of happening. The smart move in this future is similar to the smart moves now: gather capital assets and build your communtiy of family and friends to try to simulate that absent baseline of meeting your needs WHILE swinging for the fences to achieve your dreams.

I think that it is fine to quibble with some of my likelihood weights - I don't even think I got them completely correct. But, the thing that I notice is that there are many ways to arrive at the dark future that many working class Americans fear. And, there are many ways to arrive and the abundant future the techno-optimists dream about. The cognitive trap is to think that these fantastical futures are more likely to happen because there are more ways to get to them. Terminators aren't going to enslave us all. But, Star Trek is less likely to happen. Things are (probably) going to keep on keepin' on, and there is some comfort in that notion.

The career advice that I'm giving my teenager is that we will help him as much as we can, but he needs to work hard, get back up if he fails, and keep trying to reach his dreams.
